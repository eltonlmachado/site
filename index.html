ELTON MACHADO

Musician. Educator. Bassist.

About

Elton Machado is a dedicated performer and teacher who is currently a doctoral student and Graduate Assistant at the University of Southern Mississippi. Passionate about classical and popular music alike, he has found in the double and electric bass the versatility to express himself in different styles and genres.

Elton is currently the principal double bassist of the USM Symphony Orchestra. In the past, he performed with Brazilian orchestras such as the Federal University of Paraná Philharmonic Orchestra, Americana Symphony Orchestra, and Adventist University of São Paulo Symphony Orchestra. He has also performed in master classes presented by renowned double bassists such as Jeff Bradetich (USA), Giuseppe Ettorre (Italy), Gabriele Ragghianti (Italy), Marcos Machado (USA/Brazil), and Waldir Bertipaglia (Brazil).

As a popular musician, he traveled across Brazil for several years with the nationally recognized group in the Christian music scene, Novo Tom. He accompanied several Brazilian singers such as Ana Caram, Regina Mota, Pedro Valença, Riane Junqueira, Joyce Carnassale, Gabriel Iglesias, Marcel Freire, Laura Morena, and Daniel Lüdtke, as well as the American singer Jody McBrayer. In addition, Elton has participated in live and studio recording projects as a side musician, including the albums Clássicos (DVD) by Regina Mota, Pra Cima Brasil (CD/DVD) by Academia da Voz choir, and Saudade do Tempo (CD) by Fernando Iglesias.

For over four years, he served as the Adjunct Instructor of the music education department at the Adventist University of São Paulo where he taught Music Theory and Music History courses for undergraduate students. Along with it, he taught double and electric bass at the School of Arts conservatory in the same institution and, after moving to the United States, he fulfilled the function of double bass instructor at the Bass Memorial Academy in Lumberton, MS for three years.

His educational background includes a Master of Music degree from the University of Southern Mississippi, a specialization diploma in University Teaching from the Adventist University of São Paulo, and a Music Education degree from the Faculty of Arts of Paraná. Elton currently lives in Hattiesburg, MS with his wife Aline.

Music Theory

Now accepting new students.

I am glad to announce that I am now accepting new applications for double bass, electric bass, and/or music theory lessons. With more than ten years of teaching experience, I am focused on supporting students improve all aspects of their bass playing, musicality, and theory skills.

The goal of private double/electric bass lessons is to help students to express themselves musically by developing a strong foundation of the fundamentals of playing the instrument. Each lesson is tailored to the students' needs and will vary depending on his/her level and aims. In general, lessons include—but are not limited to—working on topics such as:

  • Tone quality
  • Intonation
  • Pizzicato
  • Bowing technique
  • Left-hand technique
  • Sight reading
  • Articulation
  • Vibrato
  • Rhythm accuracy
  • Scales and arpeggios
  • Phrasing
  • Etudes
  • Walking bass
  • Improvisation
  • Orchestral excerpts
  • Solo repertoire
  • Performance
